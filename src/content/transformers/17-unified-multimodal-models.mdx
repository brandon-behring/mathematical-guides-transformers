# Unified Multimodal Models

**Where we are.** The connector chapter injected continuous visual tokens into a language model; the tokenization chapter turned images into discrete sequences. A **unified multimodal model** takes the second idea to its conclusion: put text and image symbols into *one* interleaved stream and run a single decoder-only transformer (<XRef id="def-tf7-decoder-only" />) over it, predicting the next symbol whatever its modality. The architecture is a striking confirmation of the dual-footing thesis (<XRef id="rem-tf1-modality-blind" />): once both modalities are sequences over finite alphabets, modality survives only at the two ends of the model — the embedding that reads symbols in and the head that writes them out — plus one structural touch, the mixed attention mask over image segments; the body's *weights* are the modality-blind core we have already built and analyzed. This chapter makes that precise. We define the interleaved sequence, place modality at the embedding and unembedding interfaces, and prove three things: one body realizes *every* cross-modal conditional (any-to-any); with a merged vocabulary the unified model is *literally* the autoregressive transformer of the training chapter (the Chameleon reduction); and a categorical generation head forces the generated modality to be discrete, while a merely-conditioned modality may stay continuous — the formal basis of the common continuous-in/discrete-out design. We write $u_t=(c_t,x_t)$ for a tagged unit, $c_t\in\{\mathrm{txt},\mathrm{img}\}$ for its modality, $\mathbf m_c$ for a modality offset, and $\mathcal V_{\mathrm{txt}},\mathcal V_{\mathrm{img}}$ for the two alphabets.

## The interleaved sequence

The object is one stream carrying both modalities. A unit is a symbol together with a tag saying which alphabet it came from; a document is any interleaving of text spans and image-code spans — a caption followed by its image, a page of text with figures, a dialogue with pictures.

<Theorem kind="definition" name="Interleaved multimodal sequence" id="def-tf17-interleaved">
Fix a text vocabulary $\mathcal V_{\mathrm{txt}}$ and an image-code vocabulary $\mathcal V_{\mathrm{img}}=\{1,\dots,K\}$ (the visual codebook of <XRef id="def-tf16-codebook" />). A **unit** is a pair $u_t=(c_t,x_t)$ with **modality tag** $c_t\in\{\mathrm{txt},\mathrm{img}\}$ and symbol $x_t\in\mathcal V_{c_t}$. An **interleaved sequence** $u_1,\dots,u_n$ is any concatenation of text and image-code spans in one stream. The model places a joint distribution on such sequences by the chain rule (<XRef id="def-tf9-autoregressive-lm" />),
$$
p_\theta(u_1,\dots,u_n) = \prod_{t=1}^{n} p_\theta(u_t\mid u_{<t}).
$$
<Sidenote>Early-fusion unified models of this form include CM3, Unified-IO, and Chameleon <Cite key="aghajanyan2022cm3" /><Cite key="lu2022unifiedio" /><Cite key="chameleonteam2024" />. Emu <Cite key="sun2023emu" /> and Transfusion <Cite key="zhou2024transfusion" /> keep the single interleaved stream but replace the categorical image factor with a *continuous* one (regressed embeddings; a diffusion head).</Sidenote>
</Theorem>

## Modality at the two interfaces

Modality enters when a symbol is read into the stream and when one is written out — nowhere else. Reading uses a per-modality embedding table; writing uses a per-modality head. Both are the token-embedding and softmax constructions of earlier chapters, indexed by modality.

<Theorem kind="definition" name="Modality-specific embedding" id="def-tf17-modality-embedding">
Each modality carries its own embedding table, $\mathbf E_{\mathrm{txt}}\in\R^{|\mathcal V_{\mathrm{txt}}|\times d}$ and $\mathbf E_{\mathrm{img}}\in\R^{K\times d}$, and a learned **modality offset** $\mathbf m_c\in\R^{d}$. Unit $u_t=(c_t,x_t)$ is embedded by
$$
\mathbf x_t \defeq \mathbf E_{c_t}[x_t,:] + \mathbf m_{c_t} + \mathbf P[t,:],
$$
the modality table lookup plus the offset plus a position code $\mathbf P[t,:]$ (<XRef id="def-tf1-sinusoidal-pe" />). This is the token embedding of <XRef id="def-tf1-token-embedding" /> with the single table replaced by a modality-indexed one — an instance of the embedding interface (<XRef id="def-tf1-embedding-interface" />).
</Theorem>

<Theorem kind="definition" name="Routed generation heads" id="def-tf17-routed-heads">
At each position the model produces a hidden state $\mathbf h_t$ and emits next-symbol logits through the head of the *predicted next modality*; the tag $c_{t+1}$ itself is predicted as part of the sequence (in practice via sentinel begin/end-image tokens in the merged vocabulary, or a small tag head), supplying the factor $p_\theta(c_{t+1}\mid u_{\le t})$ that completes the chain rule of <XRef id="def-tf17-interleaved" />. With unembeddings $\mathbf W^{\mathrm{out}}_{\mathrm{txt}}\in\R^{d\times|\mathcal V_{\mathrm{txt}}|}$ and $\mathbf W^{\mathrm{out}}_{\mathrm{img}}\in\R^{d\times K}$,
$$
p_\theta(x_{t+1}\mid u_{\le t}, c_{t+1}) = \softmax\!\big(\mathbf h_t \mathbf W^{\mathrm{out}}_{c_{t+1}}\big)_{x_{t+1}},
$$
a softmax over the chosen modality's alphabet. Choosing which head to apply is **modality routing**.
</Theorem>

## Any-to-any: one body, every conditional

Because the joint is the chain-rule product, conditioning on any prefix and generating any suffix uses the very same factors. Splitting the stream differently into "observed" and "to-generate" gives captioning, text-to-image, or free interleaved generation — all from one trained model.

<Theorem kind="theorem" name="A unified body realizes every cross-modal conditional" id="thm-tf17-any-to-any">
Let $f_\theta$ be a single decoder-only body over interleaved sequences (<XRef id="def-tf17-interleaved" />). For any split of a sequence into an observed prefix $u_{\le s}$ and a generated suffix $u_{>s}$,
$$
p_\theta(u_{>s}\mid u_{\le s}) = \prod_{t>s} p_\theta(u_t\mid u_{<t}),
$$
and these are the same conditional factors the model already computes. Choosing the modalities of the prefix and suffix instantiates **image-to-text** (observe image codes, generate text), **text-to-image** (observe text, generate image codes), and **interleaved** generation — all from the one body $f_\theta$, with no task-specific parameters.
</Theorem>

<Theorem kind="proof">
By the chain rule (<XRef id="def-tf9-autoregressive-lm" />) the joint factorizes as $p_\theta(u_{1:n})=\prod_{t=1}^n p_\theta(u_t\mid u_{<t})$ for the fixed left-to-right order. Conditioning on any prefix with $p_\theta(u_{\le s})>0$ and marginalizing nothing (the suffix is fully generated) gives
$$
p_\theta(u_{>s}\mid u_{\le s}) = \frac{p_\theta(u_{1:n})}{p_\theta(u_{\le s})} = \frac{\prod_{t=1}^{n} p_\theta(u_t\mid u_{<t})}{\prod_{t=1}^{s} p_\theta(u_t\mid u_{<t})} = \prod_{t=s+1}^{n} p_\theta(u_t\mid u_{<t}),
$$
the trailing factors of the same product. Each factor is a softmax over the alphabet of its position's modality (<XRef id="def-tf17-routed-heads" />) and references modality only through which embedding table and head are used — never through the body. Hence assigning the prefix to one modality and the suffix to another yields a valid conditional, in the fixed serialization order, realized by evaluating $f_\theta$ with those positions observed; image-to-text and text-to-image are two of the four ordered choices of (prefix modality, suffix modality) — the other two are the same-modality continuations — and interleaved generation is the general mixed case with no single modality per side. A conditional in the opposite order (generating a prefix from a later suffix) is obtained by presenting that serialization at training time. $\qquad\blacksquare$
</Theorem>

## The merged-vocabulary reduction

The cleanest unified model erases even the routing: merge the two alphabets into one vocabulary, stack the two tables into one, and use a single head over the union. Then there is no modality bookkeeping at all — the model is, symbol for symbol, the autoregressive transformer of the training chapter.

<Theorem kind="proposition" name="A merged-vocabulary unified model is the training-chapter transformer" id="prop-tf17-merged-vocab">
Let $\mathcal V = \mathcal V_{\mathrm{txt}}\sqcup\mathcal V_{\mathrm{img}}$ be the disjoint union, of size $|\mathcal V|=|\mathcal V_{\mathrm{txt}}|+K$, with the bijection $\iota$ sending a text token $x$ to itself and an image code $z$ to $|\mathcal V_{\mathrm{txt}}|+z$. Stack the embeddings into $\mathbf E\in\R^{|\mathcal V|\times d}$ (row $\iota(c,x)$ equal to $\mathbf E_{c}[x,:]+\mathbf m_c$) and the unembeddings into $\mathbf W^{\mathrm{out}}\in\R^{d\times|\mathcal V|}$. Then the modality-specific embedding (<XRef id="def-tf17-modality-embedding" />) is a single lookup in $\mathbf E$ — the token embedding of <XRef id="def-tf1-token-embedding" /> — and a single shared head $\softmax(\mathbf h_t\mathbf W^{\mathrm{out}})$ over $\mathcal V$ is the autoregressive transformer of <XRef id="def-tf9-autoregressive-lm" /> with $|\mathcal V|=|\mathcal V_{\mathrm{txt}}|+K$. A unified model with merged vocabulary and shared head is therefore *exactly* the training-chapter model: image and text are one language.
</Theorem>

<Theorem kind="proof">
The map $\iota:(c,x)\mapsto\iota(c,x)$ is a bijection from tagged units $\{(\mathrm{txt},x):x\in\mathcal V_{\mathrm{txt}}\}\cup\{(\mathrm{img},z):z\in\mathcal V_{\mathrm{img}}\}$ onto $\{1,\dots,|\mathcal V|\}$, because the two ranges $\{1,\dots,|\mathcal V_{\mathrm{txt}}|\}$ and $\{|\mathcal V_{\mathrm{txt}}|+1,\dots,|\mathcal V_{\mathrm{txt}}|+K\}$ are disjoint and cover $\mathcal V$. Define $\mathbf E[\iota(c,x),:]\defeq\mathbf E_c[x,:]+\mathbf m_c$; then the embedding of <XRef id="def-tf17-modality-embedding" /> is $\mathbf x_t=\mathbf E[\iota(c_t,x_t),:]+\mathbf P[t,:]$, a single table lookup, which is <XRef id="def-tf1-token-embedding" /> over $\mathcal V$. For the head, set the columns of $\mathbf W^{\mathrm{out}}$ on the text block equal to $\mathbf W^{\mathrm{out}}_{\mathrm{txt}}$ and on the image block to $\mathbf W^{\mathrm{out}}_{\mathrm{img}}$; the single softmax $\softmax(\mathbf h_t\mathbf W^{\mathrm{out}})$ is then a categorical distribution over all $|\mathcal V|$ symbols, i.e. the next-token distribution of <XRef id="def-tf9-autoregressive-lm" /> with vocabulary $\mathcal V$. The body between embedding and head is the decoder-only stack (<XRef id="def-tf7-decoder-only" />), unchanged. Every component therefore coincides with the training-chapter autoregressive transformer over a vocabulary of size $|\mathcal V_{\mathrm{txt}}|+K$, and the cross-entropy training objective (<XRef id="def-tf9-ce-loss" />) and all its properties transfer verbatim. The routed-head model of <XRef id="def-tf17-routed-heads" /> is the special case that, knowing the next modality, evaluates only the corresponding block of $\mathbf W^{\mathrm{out}}$ — a computational shortcut that agrees with the shared head when off-modality probability is driven to zero. $\qquad\blacksquare$
</Theorem>

<MarginNote variant="tip" label="Why this is the punchline">If a single softmax over a merged vocabulary *is* the text transformer, then "training a multimodal generator" and "training a language model" are the same optimization — the dual-footing thesis at full strength.</MarginNote>

## Why generation is discretized but conditioning need not be

The merged-vocabulary picture needs both modalities discrete, because the generation head is a categorical softmax — and a categorical softmax is only available over a finite alphabet. Conditioning is different: a modality the model merely *reads* is never normalized over, so it can stay continuous. This asymmetry is the formal reason behind models that condition on continuous image features (Ch 10) yet generate discrete image codes (Ch 11).

<Theorem kind="proposition" name="A categorical head forces a finite output alphabet; conditioning does not" id="prop-tf17-why-discrete">
A categorical autoregressive factor $p_\theta(x_{t+1}\mid u_{\le t})=\softmax(\mathbf o_{t+1})_{x_{t+1}}$ (<XRef id="def-tf9-autoregressive-lm" />) is normalizable in closed form only over a finite alphabet: the partition function $\sum_{x'}\exp(o_{x'})$ is a finite sum, whereas the continuous analogue $\int_{\R^{d}}\exp(f(\mathbf v))\,d\mathbf v$ is in general intractable or infinite. Hence a modality that is **generated by a categorical softmax head** must be discretized (the visual codebook of <XRef id="def-tf16-codebook" />); a continuous-output head — a normalized density, normalizing flow, or diffusion head, as in Transfusion <Cite key="zhou2024transfusion" /> — escapes discretization by replacing the categorical factor, at the cost of the closed-form softmax likelihood and its cross-entropy. A modality that is only **conditioned on** enters as keys/values in attention (<XRef id="def-tf7-cross-attention" />) or as prepended embeddings (<XRef id="def-tf15-connector" />) and is never normalized over its domain, so it may remain continuous. Modality routing (<XRef id="def-tf17-routed-heads" />) is the hard, tag-determined analogue of the soft learned gate of mixture-of-experts (<XRef id="def-tf12-moe" />): both select a sub-module per position, by an argmax tag here and a top-$k$ gate there.
</Theorem>

<Theorem kind="proof">
A categorical distribution assigns $p(x)=\exp(o_x)/Z$ with $Z=\sum_{x'}\exp(o_{x'})$; $Z$ is a finite sum exactly when the alphabet is finite, and then $p$ is a valid normalized distribution and the cross-entropy loss of <XRef id="def-tf9-ce-loss" /> is computable. Over a continuum the corresponding normalizer is $Z=\int_{\R^d}\exp(f(\mathbf v))\,d\mathbf v$, which diverges for the unbounded energies a free softmax would produce and lacks a closed form in general, so a tractable *categorical* generation factor is unavailable without discretization — establishing the output claim. (Continuous generation is still possible with a different, non-categorical head — a normalized density or diffusion model — which is the design point of Transfusion; the claim is specifically about the categorical softmax head.) For conditioning, the model uses given embeddings only through inner products in attention scores and convex value averages (<XRef id="def-tf7-cross-attention" />, <XRef id="prop-row-stochastic-convex" />); no step normalizes a probability over the conditioned modality's domain, so continuous inputs are admissible — the input claim. Finally, write the modality tag $c_{t+1}$ as a one-hot gate over the two heads; the routed head $\mathbf W^{\mathrm{out}}_{c_{t+1}}$ is the gate-selected expert. Mixture-of-experts (<XRef id="def-tf12-moe" />) selects expert FFNs by a learned top-$k$ gate; replacing the soft top-$k$ gate by the hard one-hot modality tag gives modality routing, so the two share the select-a-sub-module structure with a soft versus hard gate. $\qquad\blacksquare$
</Theorem>

## The mixed block-causal mask

A purely autoregressive unified model is fully causal — every position attends only to the past, exactly as in the attention chapter. But an image is a two-dimensional block whose codes have no natural left-to-right order, so when an image span is *given as context* (or generated as a block), one often lets its positions attend to each other in both directions while keeping causality across modality boundaries. This is a single attention mask that interpolates between the two regimes.

<Theorem kind="definition" name="Mixed block-causal mask" id="def-tf17-mixed-mask">
Partition the interleaved sequence into contiguous **segments** (maximal same-modality spans). The **mixed block-causal mask** allows position $i$ to attend to position $j$ iff
$$
M_{ij}=1 \iff \underbrace{j\le i}_{\text{causal}} \ \ \text{or}\ \ \underbrace{\text{$i$ and $j$ lie in the same image segment}}_{\text{intra-image bidirectional}}.
$$
Causality is enforced *across* segments, while positions inside one image segment may attend to one another in both directions. When the stream is all text, no positions share an *image* segment, so the second clause is empty and $M$ is the causal mask of the attention chapter (<XRef id="def-tf4-causal-mask" />); the unified model then reduces to the text decoder-only transformer.
</Theorem>

<Theorem kind="remark" name="The body is the proof of the thesis" id="rem-tf17-body-unchanged">
Across all three designs of the figure — merged single-stream, two-path, and continuous-in/discrete-out — the transformer *body* is identical and identical to the text model's: the same decoder-only blocks (<XRef id="def-tf7-decoder-only" />), the same stacking, the same universal-approximation guarantee (<XRef id="thm-tf7-universal-approx" />). What differs is only the embedding, the head, and the mask. That the body needs no change to become a state-of-the-art image–text generator is the dual-footing thesis (<XRef id="rem-tf1-modality-blind" />) discharged in full: chapters 2–4 were stated over $\R^{n\times d}$, and a unified multimodal model is what happens when their sequences are allowed to carry pixels as freely as words.
</Theorem>

## Exercises

Attempt each before expanding its solution.

**Exercise 17.1 (Prove).** Prove the any-to-any factorization (<XRef id="thm-tf17-any-to-any" />) for the special case of image captioning: with the observed prefix the image codes $u_{\le s}$ and the generated suffix the caption tokens $u_{>s}$, show the conditional is the trailing product of next-token factors, and identify the modality-dependent components — and why there are exactly two.

<SolutionBox>

By the chain rule, $p_\theta(u_{1:n})=\prod_{t=1}^n p_\theta(u_t\mid u_{<t})$. Dividing by the prefix likelihood $p_\theta(u_{\le s})=\prod_{t=1}^{s}p_\theta(u_t\mid u_{<t})$ cancels the first $s$ factors and leaves $p_\theta(u_{>s}\mid u_{\le s})=\prod_{t=s+1}^{n}p_\theta(u_t\mid u_{<t})$, the caption tokens generated left to right conditioned on the image codes. Each factor is a softmax over the text alphabet $\mathcal V_{\mathrm{txt}}$ produced by the routed text head $\mathbf W^{\mathrm{out}}_{\mathrm{txt}}$ (<XRef id="def-tf17-routed-heads" />); the body $f_\theta$ that produced $\mathbf h_t$ is modality-blind. The only modality-dependent components are the embeddings that read the image codes in and the head that writes the text out — exactly the two interfaces. $\qquad\blacksquare$

</SolutionBox>

**Exercise 17.2 (Prove).** Prove the merged-vocabulary reduction (<XRef id="prop-tf17-merged-vocab" />) by exhibiting the bijection between tagged units and merged indices and checking that the stacked embedding and head reproduce <XRef id="def-tf1-token-embedding" /> and <XRef id="def-tf9-autoregressive-lm" />.

<SolutionBox>

Define $\iota(\mathrm{txt},x)=x$ for $x\in\{1,\dots,|\mathcal V_{\mathrm{txt}}|\}$ and $\iota(\mathrm{img},z)=|\mathcal V_{\mathrm{txt}}|+z$ for $z\in\{1,\dots,K\}$. The two image and text ranges are disjoint and their union is $\{1,\dots,|\mathcal V_{\mathrm{txt}}|+K\}=\mathcal V$, so $\iota$ is a bijection. Set $\mathbf E[\iota(c,x),:]\defeq\mathbf E_c[x,:]+\mathbf m_c$: then the modality embedding $\mathbf E_{c_t}[x_t,:]+\mathbf m_{c_t}+\mathbf P[t,:]$ equals $\mathbf E[\iota(c_t,x_t),:]+\mathbf P[t,:]$, one lookup in $\mathbf E$ — precisely <XRef id="def-tf1-token-embedding" /> over $\mathcal V$. Set $\mathbf W^{\mathrm{out}}$'s text and image column blocks to $\mathbf W^{\mathrm{out}}_{\mathrm{txt}}$ and $\mathbf W^{\mathrm{out}}_{\mathrm{img}}$; then $\softmax(\mathbf h_t\mathbf W^{\mathrm{out}})$ is a categorical over $\mathcal V$, the next-token head of <XRef id="def-tf9-autoregressive-lm" />. The body is unchanged, so the unified model is the training-chapter transformer with $|\mathcal V|=|\mathcal V_{\mathrm{txt}}|+K$. $\qquad\blacksquare$

</SolutionBox>

**Exercise 17.3 (Prove).** Prove the output half of <XRef id="prop-tf17-why-discrete" />: a categorical softmax head normalizes in closed form only over a finite alphabet. Give the partition function in both the finite and continuous cases and say precisely what fails in the latter.

<SolutionBox>

A categorical head sets $p(x)=\exp(o_x)/Z$ with $Z=\sum_{x'\in\mathcal A}\exp(o_{x'})$. When $\mathcal A$ is finite, $Z$ is a finite sum, strictly positive, so $p$ is a valid normalized distribution and $-\log p(x)$ (cross-entropy, <XRef id="def-tf9-ce-loss" />) is computable in closed form. When $\mathcal A=\R^d$ the normalizer is $Z=\int_{\R^d}\exp(f(\mathbf v))\,d\mathbf v$; for an unbounded energy $f$ (which a free linear-softmax head produces) this integral diverges, and even when finite it has no closed form for general $f$, so the categorical likelihood and its gradient are unavailable. Thus a tractable categorical generation factor requires discretizing the output alphabet — exactly the role of the visual codebook (<XRef id="def-tf16-codebook" />). $\qquad\blacksquare$

</SolutionBox>

**Exercise 17.4 (Construct).** Describe a continuous-in/discrete-out unified model: which interface (Ch 10 connector or Ch 11 codebook) handles the conditioned image and which handles the generated image, and justify each choice using <XRef id="prop-tf17-why-discrete" />.

<SolutionBox>

Conditioned images enter through a **connector** (<XRef id="def-tf15-connector" />) as continuous soft tokens: they are only read — used as prepended embeddings or cross-attention keys/values — and never normalized over, so by the input half of <XRef id="prop-tf17-why-discrete" /> they may stay continuous, avoiding the lossy quantization of <XRef id="prop-tf16-rate" />. Generated images use the **discrete codebook** (<XRef id="def-tf16-codebook" />): generation runs through a categorical softmax head, which by the output half of <XRef id="prop-tf17-why-discrete" /> requires a finite alphabet, so the image must be emitted as code indices and decoded by $\Psi$. The design conditions with the higher-fidelity continuous representation and generates with the only representation a categorical head admits. $\qquad\blacksquare$

</SolutionBox>

**Exercise 17.5 (Prove).** Show the mixed block-causal mask (<XRef id="def-tf17-mixed-mask" />) reduces to the causal mask of <XRef id="def-tf4-causal-mask" /> when the stream contains no image segments, and explain why intra-image bidirectionality does not break autoregressive training across segment boundaries.

<SolutionBox>

If the stream contains no image segments, no two distinct positions share one, so the second clause "$i,j$ in the same image segment" is never satisfied; the mask reduces to $M_{ij}=1\iff j\le i$, the lower-triangular causal mask of <XRef id="def-tf4-causal-mask" />. Intra-image bidirectionality is confined *within* a segment: positions in one image block may see each other, but the clause adds no edge from a position to anything in a *later* segment, so the cross-segment relation remains causal ($j\le i$). Training still factorizes left to right over segments — a later segment never conditions on a future one — so the autoregressive objective of <XRef id="def-tf9-autoregressive-lm" /> over the segment ordering is intact; only the within-image attention pattern is relaxed, which is sound because an image block is encoded/predicted as a unit rather than one raster-ordered token at a time. $\qquad\blacksquare$

</SolutionBox>
