**Where we are.** A linear time-invariant state-space model uses fixed dynamics and therefore cannot select by content (<XRef id="prop-lti-obstruction" />). A **selective SSM** makes its step size, write, and readout depend on the current token. We show that a scalar exact-ZOH specialization recovers a logistic gate and that the general selective recurrence still admits an associative parallel scan even though convolution no longer applies. We then connect causal linear attention to a constant-size matrix recurrence, expose the selective recurrence's transfer matrix, prove its semiseparable structure, and derive State Space Duality with masked linear attention. That factorization leads to chunked evaluation, where the arithmetic-minimizing chunk size and the hardware-friendly choice need not coincide.

## The selective state-space model

An LTI SSM commits to one policy before seeing any data: the same $\discA$, the same $\discB$, at every step. Selectivity re-reads the policy at every token — the current token chooses its own step size (how much time passes, hence how much the state forgets), its own write direction, and its own readout: three learned linear maps of the token where three constants used to be.

<Theorem kind="definition" name="Selective state-space model" id="def-selective-ssm">
Fix a state dimension $d_s$ and one channel of the residual stream: at position $t$ the token is $\mathbf x_t\in\R^{d}$ and the channel's scalar input is $u_t$. A **selective SSM** replaces the fixed discretization of <XRef id="thm-zoh" /> with token-dependent quantities
$$
\Delta_t \defeq \operatorname{softplus}(\mathbf x_t\mathbf w_\Delta + b_\Delta)\in(0,\infty),
\qquad
\inputmat_t \defeq (\mathbf x_t\mathbf W_B)^\top,
\qquad
\outputmat_t \defeq (\mathbf x_t\mathbf W_C)^\top,
$$
with parameters $\mathbf w_\Delta\in\R^{d}$, $b_\Delta\in\R$, $\mathbf W_B,\mathbf W_C\in\R^{d\times d_s}$ (the rows $\mathbf x_t\mathbf W_B$, $\mathbf x_t\mathbf W_C$ carried as columns), and evolves
$$
\discA_t = e^{\Delta_t\statemat},
\qquad
\discB_t = \Delta_t\inputmat_t,
\qquad
\statevec_t = \discA_t\statevec_{t-1} + \discB_t\,u_t,
\qquad
y_t = \outputmat_t^\top\statevec_t,
$$
from $\statevec_0=\mathbf 0$. The state matrix $\statemat\in\R^{d_s\times d_s}$ — in practice diagonal with entries of negative real part, in the parameterizations descending from <XRef id="prop-legs-nplr" /> — remains input-independent.<Sidenote>Selective state spaces, the softplus step-size parameterization, and the selective-scan implementation are due to Mamba <Cite key="gu2023mamba" />.</Sidenote> In the full layer each of the $d$ channels runs this recurrence on its own $u_t$ with its own $\Delta_t$, sharing $\inputmat_t,\outputmat_t$; we fix one channel and omit the position-wise feedthrough $\feedmat u_t$, which plays no role in sequence mixing.
</Theorem>

<MarginNote variant="warning" label="First-order ZOH">$\discB_t=\Delta_t\inputmat_t$ truncates exact ZOH at first order — trustworthy only when $\Delta_t\norm{\statemat}\ll 1$; at the trace's payload ($\Delta_t\norm{\statemat}=3$) it overshoots $3.2\times$.</MarginNote>

The state factor $\discA_t=e^{\Delta_t\statemat}$ is the exact zero-order-hold solution; the write is not: exact ZOH gives $\discB_t=\statemat^{-1}(e^{\Delta_t\statemat}-\mathbf I)\inputmat_t = \Delta_t\inputmat_t + \tfrac{\Delta_t^2}{2}\statemat\inputmat_t+\cdots$, so $\Delta_t\inputmat_t$ is the first-order truncation, with relative error $O(\Delta_t\norm{\statemat})$.

The point of the construction is what it breaks — and input-dependence of $\inputmat_t,\outputmat_t$ alone already destroys linearity.

<MarginNote variant="tip" label="Self-explanation">Freeze $\Delta_t$. The selections $\inputmat_t,\outputmat_t$ still read the token. What polynomial degree in $u_1$ does $y_1$ carry — and what degree would linearity allow?</MarginNote>

<Theorem kind="proposition" name="The selective SSM is not LTI" id="prop-not-lti">
As a map from the input sequence to the output sequence, the selective SSM of <XRef id="def-selective-ssm" /> is in general **nonlinear** — homogeneity and additivity both fail — hence not LTI, and it admits no input-independent kernel $\kappa$ with $y=\kappa\ast u$: the convolution representation of <XRef id="thm-conv-recurrence" /> does not extend. In particular, the hypotheses of <XRef id="prop-lti-obstruction" /> no longer apply.
</Theorem>

<Theorem kind="proof">
**Step 1 (A cubic witness).** Take $d=d_s=1$ with $\mathbf W_B=\mathbf W_C=1$ (so $\mathbf x_t = u_t$, $\inputmat_t=\outputmat_t=u_t$), and freeze the step by choosing $\mathbf w_\Delta=0$, so $\Delta_t\equiv\Delta\defeq\operatorname{softplus}(b_\Delta)>0$. At $t=1$, from $\statevec_0=0$,
$$
\statevec_1 = \discB_1 u_1 = \Delta\,u_1\cdot u_1 = \Delta u_1^2,
\qquad
y_1 = \outputmat_1\statevec_1 = u_1\cdot\Delta u_1^2 = \Delta u_1^3 .
$$
So $y_1(\alpha u)=\alpha^3\,y_1(u)$ — with $\Delta=0.5$ and $u_1=1$ versus $u_1=2$, the outputs are $0.5$ and $4.0$, a factor $8$ where homogeneity demands $2$ — and additivity fails likewise, since $(u_1+u_1')^3\neq u_1^3+u_1'^3$ in general (linearity of the map).

**Step 2 (No kernel).** Convolution against any fixed $\kappa$ is a linear map of $u$ (linearity of $\ast$). A nonlinear map therefore equals no such convolution, whatever $\kappa$.

**Step 3 (The escape).** <XRef id="prop-lti-obstruction" /> quantifies over maps that are linear *and* time-invariant; the selective SSM sits outside that class, so the obstruction has no force here. Note precisely what died: a one-line induction shows that a zero-padded delay of the input still produces the delayed output — the coefficients travel with the content — so it is **superposition**, not shift structure, that selectivity gives up. $\qquad\blacksquare$
</Theorem>

Escaping the obstruction is necessary for content-dependent selection, not yet evidence of it. The next trace supplies the evidence — and exposes the mechanism: discretization manufactures a gate.

## Selection as gating

The task on which the obstruction was exhibited is **selective copying**: keep one payload token, ignore distractors, where *content* — not position — marks the payload.<Sidenote>The selective-copy diagnostic, on which LTI models provably fail and selective models succeed, is the discriminating task of <Cite key="gu2023mamba" />.</Sidenote> We run the smallest selective SSM that can play: one state coordinate, selection through the step size alone, and — since the payload step $\Delta_t\norm{\statemat}=3$ sits far outside the first-order regime — the **exact** ZOH write.

<MarginNote variant="pattern" label="Write-once-hold">Open the gate at the payload ($g\approx 0.95$), close it elsewhere ($g\approx 0.01$): the state writes once, then holds.</MarginNote>

<Theorem kind="example" name="Selective copying, traced" id="ex-selective-copy">
Let $d_s=1$, $\statemat=a=-1$, $\inputmat_t\equiv 1$, $\outputmat_t\equiv 1$, with exact ZOH (<XRef id="thm-zoh" />, scalar case):
$$
\discA_t = e^{-\Delta_t},
\qquad
\discB_t = \tfrac{1}{a}\bigl(e^{\Delta_t a}-1\bigr)\cdot 1 = 1-e^{-\Delta_t}
\;\defeq\; g_t,
\qquad\text{so}\qquad
\statevec_t = (1-g_t)\,\statevec_{t-1} + g_t\,u_t .
$$
The selection network fires $\Delta_t=3$ on the payload and $\Delta_t=0.01$ on distractors, giving gates
$$
g_1 = 1-e^{-3} \approx 0.9502,
\qquad
g_2 = g_3 = 1-e^{-0.01} \approx 0.00995 .
$$
Run it on $u=(1,\,-1,\,1)$ — payload $1$ at $t=1$, then sign-flipping distractors:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\statevec_1 &= 0.9502\cdot 1 = 0.9502,\\
\statevec_2 &= 0.99005\cdot 0.9502 + 0.00995\cdot(-1) = 0.9408 - 0.0100 = 0.9308,\\
\statevec_3 &= 0.99005\cdot 0.9308 + 0.00995\cdot 1 = 0.9215 + 0.0100 = 0.9315 .
\end{aligned}
$$
Unrolling, $\statevec_3 = w_1u_1+w_2u_2+w_3u_3$ with content-chosen weights
$$
w_1 = g_1(1-g_2)(1-g_3) \approx 0.9314,\qquad w_2 = g_2(1-g_3)\approx 0.0099,\qquad w_3 = g_3 \approx 0.0100 :
$$
$93\%$ of the mass sits on the payload, a weight ratio $w_1/w_3\approx 94$ in favor of the *oldest* token. No stable *scalar-state* LTI system can imitate this: at $d_s = 1$ its fixed kernel $\kappa_j = C\discA^{\,j}\discB$ has $\lvert\kappa_2\rvert = \lvert\discA\rvert^2\lvert\kappa_0\rvert < \lvert\kappa_0\rvert$ whenever $\lvert\discA\rvert<1$ — the lag-$2$ weight never exceeds the lag-$0$ weight (larger states with oscillatory modes can bend the kernel's profile, so the monotonicity argument is $d_s=1$ only) — and for LTI systems of *every* state size, the same fixed profile must serve when the payload arrives last, which <XRef id="prop-lti-obstruction" /> forbids. The honesty margin is quantitative here: at the payload, first-order ZOH would write $\Delta_1 = 3$ against the exact $0.9502$ — a $3.16\times$ overshoot — while at $\Delta=0.01$ the two differ by $0.50\%$.
</Theorem>

The coefficient pattern in the trace — hold weight and write weight summing to one — is not an accident of the numbers. In the scalar exact-ZOH model it is an identity, and the gate it produces is exactly the logistic gate of the gated RNNs.

<Theorem kind="proposition" name="Gate equivalence" id="prop-gate-equivalence">
Fix $d_s=1$, $\statemat=a=-1$, and $\inputmat_t\equiv 1$ (the write scale is absorbed into $u_t$, and a scale in $\lvert a\rvert$ rescales $\Delta_t$ — without loss of generality for the convex-gate form; the *exact logistic* identity below additionally uses $a = -1$, since for general $a<0$ the gate is $1-(1+e^{\zeta})^{a}$, a generalized sigmoid). Under exact ZOH discretization,
$$
\discA_t = e^{-\Delta_t},
\qquad
\discB_t = 1-e^{-\Delta_t},
$$
so with $g_t \defeq 1-e^{-\Delta_t}\in(0,1)$ the selective recurrence is the convex interpolation
$$
\statevec_t = (1-g_t)\,\statevec_{t-1} + g_t\,u_t ,
$$
and under the softplus parameterization of <XRef id="def-selective-ssm" /> the gate is exactly logistic:
$$
g_t = \sigma(\mathbf x_t\mathbf w_\Delta + b_\Delta).
$$
This is the GRU update equation (<XRef id="def-gru" />) with the candidate replaced by the raw input, under the gate relabeling $z_t = 1-g_t$ (the GRU's $z_t$ keeps the old state; here the decay $e^{-\Delta_t}$ does).
</Theorem>

<MarginNote variant="formula" label="Step size = gate">$g_t=\sigma(\mathbf x_t\mathbf w_\Delta+b_\Delta)$: $\Delta_t\to 0^+ \Leftrightarrow g_t\to 0$ (hold); $\Delta_t\to\infty \Leftrightarrow g_t\to 1$ (write).</MarginNote>

<Theorem kind="proof">
**Step 1 (Discretize exactly).** <XRef id="thm-zoh" /> with $A=a=-1$, $B=1$ gives $\discA_t = e^{\Delta_t a}=e^{-\Delta_t}$ and $\discB_t = a^{-1}(e^{\Delta_t a}-1)B = (-1)(e^{-\Delta_t}-1) = 1-e^{-\Delta_t}$. Since $\Delta_t>0$, both lie in $(0,1)$ and $\discA_t+\discB_t = 1$: the pair $(1-g_t,\,g_t)$ is convex, and the recurrence takes the stated interpolation form.

**Step 2 (The gate is a sigmoid).** For any $\zeta\in\R$, with $\operatorname{softplus}(\zeta)=\log(1+e^{\zeta})$,
$$
1-e^{-\operatorname{softplus}(\zeta)} = 1-\frac{1}{1+e^{\zeta}} = \frac{e^{\zeta}}{1+e^{\zeta}} = \sigma(\zeta)
\qquad\text{(exponential of a log; algebra)},
$$
so $g_t=\sigma(\mathbf x_t\mathbf w_\Delta+b_\Delta)$ exactly.

**Step 3 (Limits and the GRU reading).** $\Delta_t\to\infty$: $g_t\to 1$, $\statevec_t\to u_t$ (write and reset); $\Delta_t\to 0^+$: $g_t\to 0$, $\statevec_t\to\statevec_{t-1}$ (hold). The GRU update $\statevec_t=z_t\statevec_{t-1}+(1-z_t)\tilde{\statevec}_t$ (<XRef id="def-gru" />) matches term for term with keep gate $z_t\mapsto 1-g_t = e^{-\Delta_t}$ and candidate $\tilde{\statevec}_t\mapsto u_t$; and the state-to-state gradient $\prod_{s>t}(1-g_s)$ is the gate-controlled path of <XRef id="prop-cec" /> — the error carousel returns, its gate now learned through a step size. $\qquad\blacksquare$
</Theorem>

The first synthesis is now visible: discretizing a selective ODE derives the gate in <XRef id="def-gru" />. The step size controls how much of the previous state survives at each token.

## The scan survives; the convolution dies

The LTI recurrence has three evaluations: the stepwise recurrence, convolution by the kernel of <XRef id="thm-conv-recurrence" />, and the parallel scan of <XRef id="prop-scan" />. Selectivity removes the convolution but preserves the scan because associativity holds for three arbitrary pairs without assuming constant coefficients.

<Theorem kind="proposition" name="The selective scan" id="prop-selective-scan">
For the selective recurrence $\statevec_t=\discA_t\statevec_{t-1}+\discB_tu_t$ define per-step elements $m_t \defeq (\discA_t,\ \discB_tu_t)$. Then, with the scan operator $\bullet$ of <XRef id="def-scan-op" />:
1. $m_t\bullet m_{t-1}\bullet\cdots\bullet m_1 = \bigl(\prod_{s=1}^{t}\discA_s,\ \statevec_t\bigr)$, the product time-ordered and $\statevec_0=\mathbf 0$;
2. $\bullet$ is associative on time-varying elements — the proof of <XRef id="prop-scan" /> never uses equality of the $a$-components — so all $n$ prefix states are computable in $\lceil\log_2 n\rceil$ parallel rounds exactly as in the LTI case, and the $\Theta(\log n)$ sequential depth of <XRef id="prop-sequential-depth" /> survives selectivity;
3. no convolution face remains: there is no input-independent $\kappa$ with $y=\kappa\ast u$ (<XRef id="prop-not-lti" />), so of the three evaluation modes only recurrence and scan are left.
</Theorem>

<MarginNote variant="pattern" label="Why it survives">The operator never asks whether the $a$-slots agree across elements: associativity is an identity in three arbitrary pairs.</MarginNote>

<Theorem kind="proof">
**Step 1 (Pairs compose affine maps).** Read $(\mathbf a,\mathbf b)$ as the affine map $\statevec\mapsto \mathbf a\statevec+\mathbf b$. The operator $(\mathbf a_2,\mathbf b_2)\bullet(\mathbf a_1,\mathbf b_1)=(\mathbf a_2\mathbf a_1,\ \mathbf a_2\mathbf b_1+\mathbf b_2)$ is their composition, second applied after first: $\mathbf a_2(\mathbf a_1\statevec+\mathbf b_1)+\mathbf b_2 = (\mathbf a_2\mathbf a_1)\statevec + (\mathbf a_2\mathbf b_1+\mathbf b_2)$. By induction, $m_t\bullet\cdots\bullet m_1$ represents $\statevec\mapsto(\prod_{s\le t}\discA_s)\statevec + \statevec_t$, where the constant slot is the state reached from $\statevec_0=\mathbf 0$ — claim (1).

**Step 2 (Associativity without time-invariance).** The computation in <XRef id="prop-scan" /> evaluates both parenthesizations of three *arbitrary* pairs to $(\mathbf a_3\mathbf a_2\mathbf a_1,\ \mathbf a_3\mathbf a_2\mathbf b_1+\mathbf a_3\mathbf b_2+\mathbf b_3)$, using only associativity of the matrix product and distributivity — nowhere $\mathbf a_1=\mathbf a_2=\mathbf a_3$, and nowhere commutativity (the order $\mathbf a_3\mathbf a_2\mathbf a_1$ is preserved). Hence the same log-depth prefix schedule applies to time-varying elements. A sequential recurrence with generic dense $d_s\times d_s$ transitions costs $\Theta(n\,d_s^2)$ for the matrix–vector products. For diagonal transitions (or another product-closed structure whose composition and application cost $\Theta(d_s)$), each combine costs $\Theta(d_s)$: $O(n\,d_s\log n)$ work under the doubling schedule, and $O(n\,d_s)$ either sequentially or with a work-efficient scan <Cite key="blelloch1990prefix" />.

**Step 3 (The convolution face dies).** <XRef id="thm-conv-recurrence" /> produced $\kappa_j = \outputmat^\top\discA^{\,j}\discB$ *from constancy of the coefficients*: the coupling depended on the lag $i-j$ alone. With token-dependent $\discA_s$ the coupling $\outputmat_i^\top(\prod_{s=j+1}^{i}\discA_s)\discB_j$ depends on the absolute positions — and more fundamentally, $u\mapsto\kappa\ast u$ is linear, which <XRef id="prop-not-lti" /> rules out. $\qquad\blacksquare$
</Theorem>

Selection removes the FFT-compatible kernel, but the scan never required time-invariance. Mamba trains through this scan <Cite key="gu2023mamba" />; the next sections derive a second, matrix-shaped evaluation that connects the same recurrence to attention.

## Linear attention is a recurrent model

<XRef id="rem-linear-attention" /> replaced the softmax kernel by a feature map $\phi$ and observed that the resulting sums are shared across queries. In the causal case the observation becomes an exact equivalence: the running sums *are* a recurrent state, and linear attention *is* an RNN with matrix-valued state. As there, we treat $\phi(\mathbf k_j)$ and $\mathbf v_j$ as columns, so $\phi(\mathbf k_j)\mathbf v_j^\top$ is a $D\times d_v$ outer product, and outputs $\mathbf y_i$ are rows.

<MarginNote variant="tip" label="Self-explanation">Unroll $\mathbf S_3=\mathbf S_2+\phi(\mathbf k_3)\mathbf v_3^\top$ by hand: which $j$-indexed terms appear, with what coefficients? Now repeat with the $\gamma$-update.</MarginNote>

<Theorem kind="theorem" name="Causal linear attention is a matrix-state recurrence" id="thm-linattn-rnn">
Let $\phi:\R^{d_k}\to\R^{D}$ have entrywise positive values (e.g. $\operatorname{elu}(x)+1$), and let $\mathbf q_i,\mathbf k_i\in\R^{d_k}$, $\mathbf v_i\in\R^{d_v}$ be query, key, and value vectors. Define **causal linear attention**<Sidenote>Linear attention and its recurrent reading are due to <Cite key="katharopoulos2020transformers" />.</Sidenote>
$$
\mathbf y_i \defeq \frac{\sum_{j\le i}\inner{\phi(\mathbf q_i)}{\phi(\mathbf k_j)}\,\mathbf v_j^\top}{\sum_{j\le i}\inner{\phi(\mathbf q_i)}{\phi(\mathbf k_j)}}.
$$
1. **(Recurrent form.)** With $\mathbf S_0=\mathbf 0\in\R^{D\times d_v}$, $\mathbf z_0=\mathbf 0\in\R^{D}$ and
$$
\mathbf S_i = \mathbf S_{i-1} + \phi(\mathbf k_i)\mathbf v_i^\top,
\qquad
\mathbf z_i = \mathbf z_{i-1} + \phi(\mathbf k_i),
$$
we have $\mathbf y_i = \dfrac{\phi(\mathbf q_i)^\top\mathbf S_i}{\phi(\mathbf q_i)^\top\mathbf z_i}$ for every $i$: causal linear attention is a recurrent sequence model with state $(\mathbf S_i,\mathbf z_i)$ of fixed size $D(d_v+1) = O(D\,d_v)$, updated and read out in $O(D\,d_v)$ operations per token — $O(1)$ in the position $i$, hence $O(n\,D\,d_v)$ for the sequence.
2. **($\gamma$-decay variant.)** For $\gamma\in(0,1]$, the recurrence $\mathbf S_i=\gamma\,\mathbf S_{i-1}+\phi(\mathbf k_i)\mathbf v_i^\top$, $\mathbf z_i=\gamma\,\mathbf z_{i-1}+\phi(\mathbf k_i)$ computes the same expression with every weight $\inner{\phi(\mathbf q_i)}{\phi(\mathbf k_j)}$ multiplied by $\gamma^{\,i-j}$ — recency-decayed causal linear attention. Setting $\gamma=1$ recovers (1).
</Theorem>

<MarginNote variant="formula" label="State size">Per head: $\mathbf S\in\R^{D\times d_v}$, $\mathbf z\in\R^{D}$ — $D(d_v+1)$ numbers, constant in $n$.</MarginNote>

<Theorem kind="proof">
**Step 1 (Unroll).** By induction, $\mathbf S_i=\sum_{j\le i}\phi(\mathbf k_j)\mathbf v_j^\top$: true (emptily) at $i=0$; the update appends the $j=i$ term. Identically $\mathbf z_i=\sum_{j\le i}\phi(\mathbf k_j)$.

**Step 2 (Readout).** Scalars pass through the sum:
$$
\phi(\mathbf q_i)^\top\mathbf S_i
= \sum_{j\le i}\bigl(\phi(\mathbf q_i)^\top\phi(\mathbf k_j)\bigr)\mathbf v_j^\top
= \sum_{j\le i}\inner{\phi(\mathbf q_i)}{\phi(\mathbf k_j)}\,\mathbf v_j^\top ,
$$
and $\phi(\mathbf q_i)^\top\mathbf z_i=\sum_{j\le i}\inner{\phi(\mathbf q_i)}{\phi(\mathbf k_j)}$, a sum of positive terms (positivity of $\phi$), so the quotient is defined and equals $\mathbf y_i$.

**Step 3 (Decay unrolls to $\gamma^{\,i-j}$).** By induction, $\mathbf S_i=\sum_{j\le i}\gamma^{\,i-j}\phi(\mathbf k_j)\mathbf v_j^\top$: multiplying $\mathbf S_{i-1}$ by $\gamma$ raises each exponent $i-1-j$ to $i-j$, and the appended term carries $\gamma^{0}=1$. The readout of Step 2 then carries the factor $\gamma^{\,i-j}$ onto each weight.

**Step 4 (Cost).** Per token: one outer product and one add ($O(Dd_v)$), the readouts $\phi(\mathbf q_i)^\top\mathbf S_i$ ($O(Dd_v)$) and $\phi(\mathbf q_i)^\top\mathbf z_i$ ($O(D)$) — independent of $i$. $\qquad\blacksquare$
</Theorem>

Three consequences. The weights $\inner{\phi(\mathbf q_i)}{\phi(\mathbf k_j)}$ are nonnegative and row-normalized, so each output remains a convex combination of the values (<XRef id="prop-row-stochastic-convex" />). The recurrence consumes its prefix only — causal in the sense of <XRef id="def-causal" /> — and decodes with a state that **does not grow with $n$**: with $h$ heads it uses $hD(d_v+1)$ numbers per layer, whereas a lossless KV cache for standard MHA uses $2nd$ numbers per layer under $hd_k=hd_v=d$ (<XRef id="prop-kv-memory" />); <XRef id="prop-state-recall" /> quantifies what this compression forfeits. And the $\gamma$-variant<Sidenote>Retention <Cite key="sun2023retnet" /> is the $\gamma$-decay variant with $\phi=\mathrm{id}$ composed with per-position rotations, the normalizer dropped, and one $\gamma$ per head.</Sidenote> shows the state can *forget* — at a fixed, position-keyed exponential rate. The selective SSM, we now show, is precisely this object with the decay made *content-dependent*; seeing that requires reading the recurrence as a matrix.

## The transfer matrix is semiseparable

Attention wears its mixing object on its sleeve: the row-stochastic weight matrix of <XRef id="def-attention" />, built afresh from each input. A recurrence hides its mixing object; unrolling exposes it.

<Theorem kind="definition" name="Transfer matrix" id="def-transfer-matrix">
Fix a selective SSM, its input sequence, and hence its coefficient sequences $(\discA_t,\discB_t,\outputmat_t)$. The **transfer matrix** $\mathbf T\in\R^{n\times n}$ has entries
$$
T_{ij} \defeq
\begin{cases}
\outputmat_i^\top\Bigl(\prod_{s=j+1}^{i}\discA_s\Bigr)\discB_j & j\le i,\\[2pt]
0 & j>i,
\end{cases}
$$
so that, collecting the scalar inputs and outputs into columns $u,y\in\R^{n}$, $\;y=\mathbf T u$.
</Theorem>

That $y=\mathbf Tu$ holds is the recurrence unrolled: by induction $\statevec_i=\sum_{j\le i}\bigl(\prod_{s=j+1}^{i}\discA_s\bigr)\discB_ju_j$ (the update multiplies every term by $\discA_i$ and appends the $j=i$ term $\discB_iu_i$ with its empty product), and applying $\outputmat_i^\top$ gives row $i$. Two readings matter. $\mathbf T$ is *lower-triangular*: causality is the sparsity pattern. And $\mathbf T$ is **input-dependent** — built from the selections exactly as the attention matrix is built from the tokens; the map $u\mapsto y$ is linear only once the coefficients are frozen (<XRef id="prop-not-lti" />). Both architectures thus induce a data-dependent $n\times n$ mixing matrix; the difference is structure. Attention's matrix is unconstrained (up to row-stochasticity), while $\mathbf T$ is generated by the recurrence coefficients — $\Theta(n\,d_s^2)$ transition entries in the generic dense case, reduced to $\Theta(n\,d_s)$ for diagonal transitions, aside from the read and write vectors — and that structure has a name.

<MarginNote variant="tip" label="Self-explanation">Every route from $u_j$ to $y_i$ with $j\le p\le i$ threads the $d_s$-dimensional state $\statevec_p$. What rank can a block of such couplings then have?</MarginNote>

<Theorem kind="theorem" name="The transfer matrix is d_s-semiseparable" id="thm-semiseparable">
Call a lower-triangular matrix **$N$-semiseparable** if every submatrix $\mathbf T[\mathcal I,\mathcal J]$ whose index rectangle lies in the lower-triangular region — $\max \mathcal J\le\min \mathcal I$, in particular every strictly-sub-diagonal rectangle — has rank at most $N$.<Sidenote>Semiseparable structure is classical in numerical linear algebra; its identification with SSM transfer matrices, and the name *State Space Duality*, are due to <Cite key="dao2024ssd" />.</Sidenote> The transfer matrix of any selective SSM with state dimension $d_s$ is $d_s$-semiseparable.
</Theorem>

<Theorem kind="proof">
**Step 1 (Factor through a boundary state).** Fix $\mathcal I,\mathcal J$ with $p\defeq\min \mathcal I\ge\max \mathcal J$. For $i\in \mathcal I$, $j\in \mathcal J$ we have $j\le p\le i$, so the time-ordered product splits at $p$:
$$
\prod_{s=j+1}^{i}\discA_s=\Bigl(\prod_{s=p+1}^{i}\discA_s\Bigr)\Bigl(\prod_{s=j+1}^{p}\discA_s\Bigr)
\qquad\text{(associativity; empty products are } \mathbf I\text{)},
$$
whence $T_{ij}=\mathbf r_i^\top\mathbf c_j$ with
$$
\mathbf r_i \defeq \Bigl(\prod_{s=p+1}^{i}\discA_s\Bigr)^{\!\top}\outputmat_i\in\R^{d_s},
\qquad
\mathbf c_j \defeq \Bigl(\prod_{s=j+1}^{p}\discA_s\Bigr)\discB_j\in\R^{d_s}.
$$
Interpretation: $\mathbf c_j$ is $u_j$'s contribution to the state $\statevec_p$; $\mathbf r_i^\top$ reads that state out at time $i$.

**Step 2 (Rank bound).** Stacking the $\mathbf r_i^\top$ as rows of $\mathbf R\in\R^{\lvert \mathcal I\rvert\times d_s}$ and the $\mathbf c_j$ as columns of $\mathbf G\in\R^{d_s\times\lvert \mathcal J\rvert}$ (a letter with no standing referent — bold $\mathbf K$ stays the attention key matrix) gives $\mathbf T[\mathcal I,\mathcal J]=\mathbf R\mathbf G$, and the rank of a product is at most the inner dimension: $\operatorname{rank}\mathbf T[\mathcal I,\mathcal J]\le d_s$.

**Step 3 (Diagonal case: the cumulative-decay factorization).** If $\discA_s=\operatorname{diag}(\bar{\mathbf a}_s)$ with all entries nonzero, set the entrywise cumulative decay $\boldsymbol\alpha_t\defeq\bar{\mathbf a}_t\odot\cdots\odot\bar{\mathbf a}_1$ (with $\boldsymbol\alpha_0=\mathbf 1$). Then $\prod_{s=j+1}^{i}\discA_s=\operatorname{diag}(\boldsymbol\alpha_i\oslash\boldsymbol\alpha_j)$, where $\oslash$ is entrywise division, and for all $j\le i$
$$
T_{ij}=\inner{\outputmat_i\odot\boldsymbol\alpha_i}{\discB_j\oslash\boldsymbol\alpha_j}:
$$
one global pair of vector sequences — columns $\outputmat_i\odot\boldsymbol\alpha_i$ and $\discB_j\oslash\boldsymbol\alpha_j$ — generates the entire lower triangle as inner products, so every lower rectangle is a submatrix of a single rank-$\le d_s$ product and the pivot of Step 1 is not even needed. $\qquad\blacksquare$
</Theorem>

<MarginNote variant="computational-note" label="Underflow">The cumulative decays $\boldsymbol\alpha_t$ shrink exponentially in $t$, so the global col-times-row form underflows; implementations form decay ratios only within short chunks — one reason the block algorithm exists.</MarginNote>

A generic lower-triangular matrix has $n(n+1)/2$ free entries. Semiseparability says $\mathbf T$ can instead be applied through its $d_s$-dimensional recurrence state. With generic dense transitions that application costs $\Theta(n\,d_s^2)$; with diagonal or comparably structured transitions, it costs $\Theta(n\,d_s)$. The latter is the regime used by the selective SSM above. The recurrence *is* the rank structure, but the rank bound alone does not make a dense transition matrix cheap.

## State Space Duality

Restrict the decay to the structure practice actually uses — all state coordinates decaying at one token-chosen rate — and the transfer matrix falls apart into named pieces: the readouts stack into a query matrix, the writes into a key matrix, and the leftover scalar products form a causal decay mask. That is the duality.

<Theorem kind="theorem" name="State Space Duality (scalar decay)" id="thm-ssd">
Let the selective SSM have scalar decay $\discA_t=a_t\mathbf I_{d_s}$ with $a_t\in(0,1]$ — e.g. $a_t=e^{\Delta_t a}$ for a scalar $a<0$ — and the first-order writes $\discB_t=\Delta_t\inputmat_t$ of <XRef id="def-selective-ssm" />. Stack $\mathbf C\in\R^{n\times d_s}$ with rows $\outputmat_i^\top$, $\mathbf B\in\R^{n\times d_s}$ with rows $\inputmat_j^\top$, write $\boldsymbol\Delta\defeq(\Delta_1,\dots,\Delta_n)$, and define the **decay mask** $\mathbf L\in\R^{n\times n}$ by
$$
L_{ij}\defeq\prod_{s=j+1}^{i}a_s \quad (j\le i),\qquad L_{ij}\defeq 0 \quad (j>i),\qquad L_{ii}=1 .
$$
Then the transfer matrix factorizes as
$$
\mathbf T = \mathbf L \odot \bigl(\mathbf C\mathbf B^\top\bigr)\operatorname{diag}(\boldsymbol\Delta),
$$
and $\mathbf L$ is $1$-semiseparable. Consequently $y=\mathbf Tu$ is **masked linear attention**: scores $\inner{\outputmat_i}{\inputmat_j}$ with queries the readout selections and keys the write selections, values $\Delta_ju_j$, and a multiplicative, data-dependent causal mask $\mathbf L$ — with no softmax and no row normalization. Two specializations tie back exactly: $a_s\equiv 1$ makes $\mathbf L$ the $0/1$ causal indicator (the entrywise exponential of the additive mask $\mathbf M$, <XRef id="def-causal-mask" />) and $\mathbf T u$ unnormalized causal linear attention with $\phi=\mathrm{id}$; $a_s\equiv\gamma$ gives $L_{ij}=\gamma^{\,i-j}$, the retention decay of <XRef id="thm-linattn-rnn" />(2).
</Theorem>

<Theorem kind="proof">
**Step 1 (Unroll).** With $\discA_s=a_s\mathbf I$ the time-ordered products collapse to scalars, $\prod_{s=j+1}^{i}\discA_s=\bigl(\prod_{s=j+1}^{i}a_s\bigr)\mathbf I$, so by induction on $i$ (base $\statevec_1=\discB_1u_1$; the update scales every term by $a_i$ and appends the $j=i$ term with empty product $1$)
$$
\statevec_i=\sum_{j\le i}\Bigl(\prod_{s=j+1}^{i}a_s\Bigr)\Delta_j\inputmat_j\,u_j .
$$

**Step 2 (Identify the entries).** Applying $\outputmat_i^\top$,
$$
y_i=\sum_{j\le i}\Bigl(\prod_{s=j+1}^{i}a_s\Bigr)\bigl(\outputmat_i^\top\inputmat_j\bigr)\Delta_j\,u_j
=\sum_{j}\Bigl[\mathbf L\odot\bigl(\mathbf C\mathbf B^\top\bigr)\operatorname{diag}(\boldsymbol\Delta)\Bigr]_{ij}u_j ,
$$
since $\bigl[(\mathbf C\mathbf B^\top)\operatorname{diag}(\boldsymbol\Delta)\bigr]_{ij}=(\outputmat_i^\top\inputmat_j)\Delta_j$ and the Hadamard factor $L_{ij}$ supplies the decay product below the diagonal and the zero above it. Both sides vanish for $j>i$, so the matrices agree entrywise: $\mathbf T=\mathbf L\odot(\mathbf C\mathbf B^\top)\operatorname{diag}(\boldsymbol\Delta)$.

**Step 3 ($\mathbf L$ is $1$-semiseparable).** With $\alpha_i\defeq\prod_{s\le i}a_s>0$, every lower-triangular entry is a ratio, $L_{ij}=\alpha_i/\alpha_j$, so any rectangle with $\max \mathcal J\le\min \mathcal I$ is a submatrix of the outer product $(\alpha_i)_i\,(1/\alpha_j)_j^\top$ — rank at most $1$. (Equivalently: $\mathbf L$ is itself the transfer matrix of the scalar SSM with $d_s=1$, $\discB=\outputmat=1$, so <XRef id="thm-semiseparable" /> applies with $d_s=1$.)

**Step 4 (Specializations).** If $a_s\equiv 1$, every product is $1$: $\mathbf L$ is the lower-triangular all-ones matrix, i.e. $e^{M_{ij}}$ entrywise, and $\mathbf Tu=\bigl(\text{causal mask}\odot\mathbf C\mathbf B^\top\bigr)\operatorname{diag}(\boldsymbol\Delta)u$ — the unnormalized form of <XRef id="thm-linattn-rnn" /> with $\phi=\mathrm{id}$, queries $\outputmat_i$, keys $\inputmat_j$, values $\Delta_ju_j$. If $a_s\equiv\gamma$, then $L_{ij}=\gamma^{\,i-j}$, matching Step 3 of that theorem's proof. $\qquad\blacksquare$
</Theorem>

<MarginNote variant="formula" label="The mask family">$a_s\equiv 1$: $\mathbf L$ = causal $0/1$ mask. $a_s\equiv\gamma$: $L_{ij}=\gamma^{\,i-j}$ (retention). Token-chosen $a_s$: learned, content-keyed recency.</MarginNote>

The correspondence, element by element:

| Attention object | Selective-SSM object |
|---|---|
| query $\mathbf q_i$ | readout selection $\outputmat_i$ |
| key $\mathbf k_j$ | write selection $\inputmat_j$ |
| value $\mathbf v_j$ | scaled input $\Delta_j u_j$ |
| additive $0/{-\infty}$ mask $\mathbf M$ | multiplicative decay mask $\mathbf L$ (data-dependent, $1$-semiseparable) |
| softmax row normalization | — |

<MarginNote variant="analogy-limit" label="Where it breaks">No softmax, no normalization: score rows need not sum to one, so outputs are not convex combinations of the values.</MarginNote>

The correspondence breaks in exactly one row: nothing normalizes. The decay mask tempers scores without a softmax, and an optional normalizer follows by running the augmented recurrence with values $\equiv 1$ — the $\mathbf z$-state of <XRef id="thm-linattn-rnn" />, which SSD-style models omit. The duality is therefore a statement about *algorithms*: one function, two evaluation orders. As a recurrence (or scan), $y=\mathbf Tu$ costs $O(n\,d_s)$ per channel; as a masked matmul it costs $O(n^2)$-scale work like attention — and both produce the same values. The practical algorithm interpolates.

## The chunked block algorithm

Near the diagonal the transfer matrix is dense but *small* if tiled; everything strictly below the tiles is rank-$\le d_s$, generated by boundary states. So tile it: dense masked matmuls on the $c\times c$ diagonal blocks, and one short scan over $n/c$ chunk summaries to carry the state across boundaries.

<Theorem kind="proposition" name="Chunked evaluation: cost and balance" id="prop-chunked">
Let the SSD model of <XRef id="thm-ssd" /> act on $d$ channels sharing $(a_t,\Delta_t,\inputmat_t,\outputmat_t)$ — the Mamba-2/SSD coarsening of <XRef id="def-selective-ssm" />'s per-channel $\Delta_t$, and exactly what lets one mask $\mathbf L$ serve all $d$ channels — with value block $\mathbf U\in\R^{n\times d}$, and let $c\mid n$ split positions into chunks $\mathcal I_k=\{(k-1)c+1,\dots,kc\}$ with boundaries $p_k\defeq(k-1)c$, $k=1,\dots,n/c$. Then for $t\in\mathcal I_k$,
$$
y_t=\underbrace{\sum_{j\in\mathcal I_k,\,j\le t} T_{tj}\,u_j}_{\text{intra-chunk}}
\;+\;
\underbrace{\outputmat_t^\top\Bigl(\prod_{s=p_k+1}^{t}a_s\Bigr)\statevec_{p_k}}_{\text{cross-chunk}},
$$
where the boundary states obey the chunk-level recurrence $\statevec_{p_{k+1}}=\Pi_k\statevec_{p_k}+\mathbf w_k$ with
$$
\Pi_k\defeq\prod_{s\in\mathcal I_k}a_s,
\qquad
\mathbf w_k\defeq\sum_{j\in\mathcal I_k}\Bigl(\prod_{s\in\mathcal I_k,\,s>j}a_s\Bigr)\Delta_j\inputmat_j\,u_j .
$$
The resulting algorithm costs, counting multiplications across all $d$ channels (boundary states stacked as $\statevec_{p_k}\in\R^{d_s\times d}$):
1. **intra-chunk masked matmuls** — the diagonal blocks of $\mathbf T$ in SSD form: $O(c^2(d_s+d))$ per chunk, total $O\bigl(n\,c(d_s+d)\bigr)$;
2. **chunk summaries and boundary broadcasts**: $O(c\,d_s\,d)$ per chunk, total $O(n\,d_s\,d)$;
3. **the cross-chunk scan** on the pairs $(\Pi_k,\mathbf w_k)$: $n/c$ combines at $O(d_s\,d)$ each, total $O\bigl((n/c)\,d_s\,d\bigr)$ — sequential, or $\lceil\log_2(n/c)\rceil$ rounds by <XRef id="prop-selective-scan" />.

Ignoring lower-order mask bookkeeping but retaining the $\mathbf C\mathbf B^\top$ formation, a scalar-multiplication proxy for these three terms is
$$
P(c)=n\,c(d+d_s)+2n\,d_s d+\frac{n}{c}d_s d.
$$
Its unconstrained positive minimizer is
$$
c_* = \sqrt{\frac{d_s d}{d+d_s}},
$$
which approaches $\sqrt{d_s}$ only in the wide-channel regime $d\gg d_s$. The continuous legal target is $\widehat c_*=\min(n,\max(1,c_*))$. Among divisors of $n$, the proxy is minimized by the divisor closest to $\widehat c_*$ **multiplicatively** (minimizing $|\log(c/\widehat c_*)|$), not necessarily the one with smallest additive distance. For $d_s\le d$, every admissible $1\le c\le\min(d_s,n)$ lies on the same $\Theta(n\,d_s\,d)$ asymptotic plateau. When $d_s\le n$ and a divisor exists at that scale, the hardware-shaped choice $c=\Theta(d_s)$ equalizes the dense intra-chunk scale (1) with the summary and broadcast scale (2) and makes the scan term lower-order; it is not the scalar-count minimizer. The endpoints recover the duality's two faces: $c=n$ is the full masked matmul at $O\bigl(n^2(d+d_s)\bigr)=O(n^2d)$ for $d_s\le d$, and $c=1$ the pure recurrence at $O(n\,d_s\,d)$.
</Theorem>

<MarginNote variant="pattern" label="Two balances">$\widehat c_*=\operatorname{clamp}_{[1,n]}\sqrt{d_sd/(d+d_s)}$ minimizes the legal continuous multiplication proxy. In the interior wide-channel regime it is $\approx\sqrt{d_s}$; when $d_s\le n$, $c\asymp d_s$ favors larger dense blocks on the same $\Theta(nd_sd)$ plateau.</MarginNote>

<Theorem kind="proof">
**Step 1 (Split the sum at the chunk boundary).** For $t\in\mathcal I_k$, split $y_t=\sum_{j\le t}T_{tj}u_j$ into $j\in\mathcal I_k$ and $j\le p_k$. In the second sum, factor the decay at $p_k$ (associativity of the product, $j\le p_k< t$):
$$
\sum_{j\le p_k}\Bigl(\prod_{s=j+1}^{t}a_s\Bigr)\bigl(\outputmat_t^\top\inputmat_j\bigr)\Delta_ju_j
=\outputmat_t^\top\Bigl(\prod_{s=p_k+1}^{t}a_s\Bigr)\underbrace{\sum_{j\le p_k}\Bigl(\prod_{s=j+1}^{p_k}a_s\Bigr)\Delta_j\inputmat_ju_j}_{=\;\statevec_{p_k}} ,
$$
the underbraced sum being the unrolled state at time $p_k$ (Step 1 of <XRef id="thm-ssd" />) — which is the cross-chunk term. The intra-chunk sum is the diagonal block of $\mathbf T$ applied to the chunk's inputs — a chunk-sized instance of <XRef id="thm-ssd" /> with zero initial state.

**Step 2 (Boundary states are a scan).** Grouping the unrolled $\statevec_{p_{k+1}}$ by whether $j\le p_k$ or $j\in\mathcal I_k$: every early term acquires the whole chunk's decay $\Pi_k$, and the in-chunk terms sum to $\mathbf w_k$; hence $\statevec_{p_{k+1}}=\Pi_k\statevec_{p_k}+\mathbf w_k$. This is a length-$(n/c)$ recurrence in the elements $(\Pi_k,\mathbf w_k)$, evaluable sequentially or by $\bullet$ — associativity holding by <XRef id="prop-selective-scan" />.

**Step 3 (Count).** *(1)* Diagonal block $k$: form $\mathbf C^{(k)}\mathbf B^{(k)\top}$ from the chunk's rows of $\mathbf C,\mathbf B$ ($c^2d_s$ multiplications), apply the chunk's mask $\mathbf L^{(k)}$ (lower-order $O(c^2)$ bookkeeping), and multiply into the chunk's value block in $\R^{c\times d}$ ($c^2d$ multiplications). Across $n/c$ chunks, the two matrix products therefore contribute $nc(d_s+d)$ multiplications. *(2)* Each $\mathbf w_k$ is $\mathbf B^{(k)\top}$ times the chunk's decay-and-$\Delta$-scaled values ($c\,d_s\,d$ multiplications); each broadcast is $\mathbf C^{(k)}$ (decay-scaled) times $\statevec_{p_k}\in\R^{d_s\times d}$ (another $c\,d_s\,d$), totaling $2n\,d_s\,d$. *(3)* Each combine scales a $d_s\times d$ state, contributing $d_s d$ multiplications, for the proxy $(n/c)d_s d$ across the chunk-level scan. Summing gives the stated $P(c)$; operation fusion and the fact that a length-$n/c$ sequential scan has one fewer combine affect constants or endpoints, not the balance.

**Step 4 (Arithmetic optimum, hardware shape, and endpoints).** Only $c(d+d_s)+d_s d/c$ varies with $c$. By AM–GM,
$$
c(d+d_s)+\frac{d_s d}{c}\ge2\sqrt{d_s d(d+d_s)},
$$
with equality at $c=c_*$. Writing $c=c_*e^u$ makes the variable term $2\sqrt{d_s d(d+d_s)}\cosh u$. Restricting first to $1\le c\le n$ clamps the target to $\widehat c_*$; among divisors of $n$, strict monotonicity of $\cosh |u|$ makes the optimum exactly the divisor with smallest $|\log(c/\widehat c_*)|$. The constant $2n d_s d$ term is a floor. When $d_s\le d$ and $1\le c\le\min(d_s,n)$, the intra term is at most $2n d_s d$ and the scan term at most $n d_s d$, while the constant state terms supply a matching lower bound; hence the total remains $\Theta(n\,d_s\,d)$. If also $d_s\le n$ and the divisor constraint admits $c=\Theta(d_s)$, that hardware-shaped choice is asymptotically optimal: it equalizes the intra-chunk and state-summary scales and pushes work into larger dense products, although it does more scalar multiplications than the interior $c_*\approx\sqrt{d_s}$ when $d\gg d_s$. At $c=n$ there is one chunk, so cross-chunk summaries, broadcasts, and scans can be omitted; forming the full $\mathbf C\mathbf B^\top$ factor and multiplying the masked matrix by $\mathbf U$ costs $O\bigl(n^2(d_s+d)\bigr)$ (the attention face). At $c=1$ the diagonal blocks are trivial and the scan is the whole algorithm (the recurrent face). $\qquad\blacksquare$
</Theorem>

Why choose the hardware-shaped middle of the plateau rather than the scalar-count minimizer? Because the *shape* of the arithmetic changes: larger chunks spend more operations in dense $c\times c$ and $c\times d$ products — high arithmetic intensity, the compute-bound side of <XRef id="def-cost-eq" /> — where the elementwise sequential scan streams its state through memory. The asymptotic count stays fixed while the exact count and achieved throughput change; this matmul-rich reorganization is the training-time algorithm of <Cite key="dao2024ssd" />. Thus a selective SSM escapes the LTI obstruction, retains the scan, equals masked linear attention under a semiseparable mask, and can be evaluated between recurrent and attention endpoints. <XRef id="prop-state-recall" /> next identifies what its constant-size state cannot remember exactly.

**Exercise 18.1 (Prove).** Show that $(\mathbf a_2,\mathbf b_2)\bullet(\mathbf a_1,\mathbf b_1)=(\mathbf a_2\mathbf a_1,\ \mathbf a_2\mathbf b_1+\mathbf b_2)$ composes the affine maps $\statevec\mapsto\mathbf a\statevec+\mathbf b$; verify associativity on three arbitrary (time-varying) pairs; and state exactly where an LTI assumption would have been needed.

<SolutionBox exercise="18.1">

Associativity: $\bigl((\mathbf a_3,\mathbf b_3)\bullet(\mathbf a_2,\mathbf b_2)\bigr)\bullet(\mathbf a_1,\mathbf b_1)=(\mathbf a_3\mathbf a_2,\ \mathbf a_3\mathbf b_2+\mathbf b_3)\bullet(\mathbf a_1,\mathbf b_1)=(\mathbf a_3\mathbf a_2\mathbf a_1,\ \mathbf a_3\mathbf a_2\mathbf b_1+\mathbf a_3\mathbf b_2+\mathbf b_3)$, and $(\mathbf a_3,\mathbf b_3)\bullet\bigl((\mathbf a_2,\mathbf b_2)\bullet(\mathbf a_1,\mathbf b_1)\bigr)=(\mathbf a_3,\mathbf b_3)\bullet(\mathbf a_2\mathbf a_1,\ \mathbf a_2\mathbf b_1+\mathbf b_2)=(\mathbf a_3\mathbf a_2\mathbf a_1,\ \mathbf a_3\mathbf a_2\mathbf b_1+\mathbf a_3\mathbf b_2+\mathbf b_3)$ — equal. Only associativity of the matrix product and distributivity were used; the $\mathbf a_i$ were never assumed equal (time-invariance) nor commuting. An LTI assumption is needed nowhere — which is <XRef id="prop-selective-scan" />. $\qquad\blacksquare$

</SolutionBox>

**Exercise 18.2 (Derive).** From exact ZOH with $a=-1$, $B_t\equiv 1$, derive $\discA_t=e^{-\Delta_t}$ and $\discB_t=1-e^{-\Delta_t}$; prove $1-e^{-\operatorname{softplus}(\zeta)}=\sigma(\zeta)$ for all $\zeta\in\R$; and give the recurrence's limits as $\Delta_t\to 0^+$ and $\Delta_t\to\infty$.

<SolutionBox exercise="18.2">

<XRef id="thm-zoh" /> in the scalar case gives $\discA_t=e^{\Delta_t a}=e^{-\Delta_t}$ and $\discB_t=a^{-1}(e^{\Delta_t a}-1)B=(-1)(e^{-\Delta_t}-1)=1-e^{-\Delta_t}$, so $\statevec_t=(1-g_t)\statevec_{t-1}+g_tu_t$ with $g_t=1-e^{-\Delta_t}$. Sigmoid identity: $e^{-\operatorname{softplus}(\zeta)}=e^{-\log(1+e^{\zeta})}=(1+e^{\zeta})^{-1}$, hence $1-e^{-\operatorname{softplus}(\zeta)}=e^{\zeta}/(1+e^{\zeta})=\sigma(\zeta)$. Limits: $\Delta_t\to 0^+$ gives $g_t\to 0$, $\statevec_t\to\statevec_{t-1}$ (hold, the token is ignored); $\Delta_t\to\infty$ gives $g_t\to 1$, $\statevec_t\to u_t$ (write, the state resets to the input) — <XRef id="prop-gate-equivalence" />. $\qquad\blacksquare$

</SolutionBox>

**Exercise 18.3 (Prove).** For the $\gamma$-decay recurrence $\mathbf S_i=\gamma\mathbf S_{i-1}+\phi(\mathbf k_i)\mathbf v_i^\top$ with $\mathbf S_0=\mathbf 0$, prove by induction that $\mathbf S_i=\sum_{j\le i}\gamma^{\,i-j}\phi(\mathbf k_j)\mathbf v_j^\top$, and identify which $\gamma$ recovers the running-sum state of <XRef id="thm-linattn-rnn" />(1) and which mask entries of <XRef id="thm-ssd" /> this corresponds to.

<SolutionBox exercise="18.3">

Base $i=0$: both sides are $\mathbf 0$ (empty sum). Step: assuming the formula at $i-1$,
$$
\mathbf S_i=\gamma\sum_{j\le i-1}\gamma^{\,i-1-j}\phi(\mathbf k_j)\mathbf v_j^\top+\phi(\mathbf k_i)\mathbf v_i^\top
=\sum_{j\le i-1}\gamma^{\,i-j}\phi(\mathbf k_j)\mathbf v_j^\top+\gamma^{0}\phi(\mathbf k_i)\mathbf v_i^\top
=\sum_{j\le i}\gamma^{\,i-j}\phi(\mathbf k_j)\mathbf v_j^\top .
$$
Readout against $\phi(\mathbf q_i)$ multiplies each weight by $\gamma^{\,i-j}$. $\gamma=1$ makes every coefficient $1$ — the running sum of part (1). In the SSD picture this is the constant-decay mask $L_{ij}=\gamma^{\,i-j}$ ($a_s\equiv\gamma$), and $\gamma=1$ the all-ones causal mask. $\qquad\blacksquare$

</SolutionBox>

**Exercise 18.4 (Prove).** For diagonal $\discA_s=\operatorname{diag}(\bar{\mathbf a}_s)$ with strictly positive entries, define $\boldsymbol\alpha_t\defeq\bar{\mathbf a}_t\odot\cdots\odot\bar{\mathbf a}_1$ and prove that every submatrix of $\mathbf T$ with index rectangle in the lower triangle has rank at most $d_s$, by exhibiting the generating inner-product form $T_{ij}=\inner{\outputmat_i\odot\boldsymbol\alpha_i}{\discB_j\oslash\boldsymbol\alpha_j}$. Where is positivity (or at least nonvanishing) of the entries used?

<SolutionBox exercise="18.4">

For $j\le i$, $\prod_{s=j+1}^{i}\discA_s=\operatorname{diag}(\boldsymbol\alpha_i\oslash\boldsymbol\alpha_j)$, since coordinatewise $\prod_{s=j+1}^{i}\bar a_{s,m}=\alpha_{i,m}/\alpha_{j,m}$ — the division defined because every $\bar a_{s,m}>0$ forces $\alpha_{j,m}\neq 0$ (this is where positivity enters). Then
$$
T_{ij}=\outputmat_i^\top\operatorname{diag}(\boldsymbol\alpha_i\oslash\boldsymbol\alpha_j)\discB_j
=\sum_{m=1}^{d_s} C_{i,m}\,\alpha_{i,m}\,\frac{1}{\alpha_{j,m}}\,\bar B_{j,m}
=\inner{\outputmat_i\odot\boldsymbol\alpha_i}{\discB_j\oslash\boldsymbol\alpha_j}.
$$
Stack rows $(\outputmat_i\odot\boldsymbol\alpha_i)^\top$ over $i\in \mathcal I$ into $\widetilde{\mathbf R}\in\R^{\lvert \mathcal I\rvert\times d_s}$ and columns $\discB_j\oslash\boldsymbol\alpha_j$ over $j\in \mathcal J$ into $\widetilde{\mathbf G}\in\R^{d_s\times\lvert \mathcal J\rvert}$; if the rectangle $\mathcal I\times \mathcal J$ lies in the lower triangle, every entry of $\mathbf T[\mathcal I,\mathcal J]$ is the corresponding entry of $\widetilde{\mathbf R}\widetilde{\mathbf G}$, whose rank is at most the inner dimension $d_s$ — <XRef id="thm-semiseparable" />, Step 3. $\qquad\blacksquare$

</SolutionBox>

**Exercise 18.5 (Compute).** Take $n=3$, $d_s=1$, $a=-1$, $\Delta=(\ln 2,\ \ln 4,\ \ln 2)\approx(0.6931,\,1.3863,\,0.6931)$ (so $a_t=e^{-\Delta_t}=(0.5,\,0.25,\,0.5)$), $B=(1,-1,2)$, $C=(1,2,1)$, $u=(2,1,-1)$, with first-order writes $\discB_t=\Delta_tB_t$. Compute $y$ (a) by the recurrence and (b) by materializing $\mathbf T=\mathbf L\odot(\mathbf C\mathbf B^\top)\operatorname{diag}(\boldsymbol\Delta)$, and verify they agree.

<SolutionBox exercise="18.5">

**(a) Recurrence** $\statevec_t=a_t\statevec_{t-1}+\Delta_tB_tu_t$, $y_t=C_t\statevec_t$:
$\statevec_1=0.6931\cdot 1\cdot 2=1.3863$, $y_1=1.3863$;
$\statevec_2=0.25\cdot 1.3863+1.3863\cdot(-1)\cdot 1=0.3466-1.3863=-1.0397$, $y_2=2\cdot(-1.0397)=-2.0794$;
$\statevec_3=0.5\cdot(-1.0397)+0.6931\cdot 2\cdot(-1)=-0.5199-1.3863=-1.9062$, $y_3=-1.9062$.

**(b) Matrix.** $L_{21}=a_2=0.25$, $L_{31}=a_3a_2=0.125$, $L_{32}=a_3=0.5$, diagonal $1$:
$$
\mathbf L=\begin{pmatrix}1&0&0\\ 0.25&1&0\\ 0.125&0.5&1\end{pmatrix},
\qquad
\mathbf T=\mathbf L\odot(\mathbf C\mathbf B^\top)\operatorname{diag}(\boldsymbol\Delta)
=\begin{pmatrix}0.6931&0&0\\ 0.3466&-2.7726&0\\ 0.0866&-0.6931&1.3863\end{pmatrix},
$$
e.g. $T_{31}=L_{31}\,C_3B_1\,\Delta_1=0.125\cdot 1\cdot 0.6931=0.0866$ and $T_{22}=C_2B_2\Delta_2=2\cdot(-1)\cdot 1.3863=-2.7726$. Then
$$
\mathbf Tu=\bigl(0.6931\cdot 2,\ \ 0.3466\cdot 2-2.7726,\ \ 0.0866\cdot 2-0.6931-1.3863\bigr)=(1.3863,\ -2.0794,\ -1.9062),
$$
matching (a) to rounding — <XRef id="thm-ssd" /> in numbers. $\qquad\blacksquare$

**Exercise 18.6 (Compute).** For $n=8192$, $d=1024$, $d_s=16$, use the multiplication proxy $P(c)$ of <XRef id="prop-chunked" /> to count intra-chunk products, summaries + broadcasts, and scan products at $c=4$, $c=16$, and $c=256$. Identify the arithmetic-minimizing admissible chunk size, explain why $c=16$ is still a hardware-shaped choice on the same asymptotic plateau, and compare both with the $c=n$ masked-matmul mode.

<SolutionBox exercise="18.6">

The summary and broadcast terms are independent of $c$: each is $n\,d_s\,d=8192\cdot16\cdot1024=134{,}217{,}728$, together $268{,}435{,}456$. The intra term is $nc(d+d_s)$ and the scan term is $(n/c)d_sd$:

| $c$ | intra | summaries + broadcasts | scan | total $P(c)$ |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| $4$ | $34{,}078{,}720$ | $268{,}435{,}456$ | $33{,}554{,}432$ | $336{,}068{,}608$ |
| $16$ | $136{,}314{,}880$ | $268{,}435{,}456$ | $8{,}388{,}608$ | $413{,}138{,}944$ |
| $256$ | $2{,}181{,}038{,}080$ | $268{,}435{,}456$ | $524{,}288$ | $2{,}449{,}997{,}824$ |

The continuous optimum is $c_*=\sqrt{16\cdot1024/(1024+16)}\approx3.969$; among divisors of $8192$, $c=4$ is the multiplicatively closest and therefore minimizes $P$. The $c=d_s=16$ choice instead makes the intra term comparable to each state term and gives larger dense blocks; both $c=4$ and $c=16$ lie on the $\Theta(n\,d_s\,d)$ plateau. In the $c=n$ mode the cross-chunk terms disappear, while forming $\mathbf C\mathbf B^\top$ and multiplying by $\mathbf U$ costs $n^2(d+d_s)=69{,}793{,}218{,}560$ multiplications under the same proxy — about $207.7\times$ the $c=4$ total and $168.9\times$ the $c=16$ total. $\qquad\blacksquare$

</SolutionBox>
